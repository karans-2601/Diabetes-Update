Insulin Pump Therapy Improves Cardiometabolic Health in Newly Diagnosed Type 2 Diabetes

March 4, 2026

Key Takeaways

  • Early intensive insulin pump therapy (CSII) in newly diagnosed Type 2 diabetes may significantly improve cardiometabolic health.
  • The therapy has been shown to reduce the cardiometabolic index, indicating better metabolic control.
  • It may also lower insulin resistance, improving the body's response to insulin.
  • Pancreatic β-cell function may improve, suggesting potential preservation of endogenous insulin secretion.
  • These findings highlight the metabolic advantages of early intensive glycemic management using continuous subcutaneous insulin infusion (CSII).

Comparative Clinical Effects of Oral Semaglutide and SGLT2 Inhibitors in Type 2 Diabetes

March 4, 2026

Key Takeaways

  • Oral semaglutide and SGLT2 inhibitors demonstrate comparable efficacy in improving glycemic control in patients with type 2 diabetes.
  • Both therapies are also associated with similar reductions in body weight.
  • However, treatment with SGLT2 inhibitors has been linked to a reduction in skeletal muscle mass in some patients.
  • This finding highlights the importance of monitoring body composition during therapy.
  • Additional caution may be required in patients at risk of sarcopenia or frailty, where muscle loss could have clinical implications.
